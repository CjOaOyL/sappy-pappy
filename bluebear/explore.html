Where You Are

The Adirondacks: America's Wild Heart

Hadley, New York sits on the edge of the Adirondack Park — six million acres of protected wilderness, clean lakes, rolling peaks, and a sky that stretches further than you'd expect. From Blue Bear Cottage, you're close to everything that makes this region legendary, yet far enough that the quiet is real. These are not simulated nature experiences. This is the real thing.

🌊

Great Sacandaga Lake

2 miles from the cottage

One of New York's largest reservoirs, stretching nearly 30 miles with sandy beaches and warm summer water. Great Sacandaga is perfect for swimming on a July afternoon, fishing for walleye at dawn, or renting a pontoon and spending the whole day on the water. Easy to love, hard to leave.

Swimming Fishing Boating Kayaking
⛷️

West Mountain Ski Area

~30 minutes away

A beloved family mountain with 27 trails for all skill levels, night skiing, and a genuine local feel that the big resorts can't replicate. Whether you're putting a beginner on skis for the first time or carving hard groomers, West Mountain delivers the kind of day that earns a good fire that night.

Skiing Snowboarding Night Skiing Lessons
🏛️

Saratoga Springs

~30 minutes away

A historic spa city with mineral springs, world-class thoroughbred racing at Saratoga Race Course, and a downtown lined with excellent restaurants, galleries, and shops. Spend a Saturday at the track, then wander Congress Park in the evening. Saratoga is sophisticated without trying too hard.

Horse Racing Dining Spas Arts & Culture
👑

Lake George

~30 minutes away

Called the "Queen of American Lakes," Lake George is stunning — crystal-clear water surrounded by forested peaks, with a charming village and endless ways to get on the water. Take a boat tour, rent a kayak, or just walk the shoreline and let the view do its work. Worth every visit.

Boat Tours Beaches Hiking Village
🏔️

Hadley Mountain

Local / walking distance

The classic local hike — a moderately challenging climb through hardwood forest to a restored fire tower at the summit, where the views stretch across the Adirondacks in every direction. On a clear day you can see the High Peaks, Sacandaga Lake, and beyond. This one stays with you.

Hiking Fire Tower Summit Views All Skill Levels
🛶

Hudson River Paddling

Nearby access points

The upper Hudson River winds through some of the most scenic terrain in the Northeast. Put in a kayak or canoe and let the current take you past herons, beaver dams, and stands of old-growth hemlock. Outfitter rentals are available close by — no gear required, just the urge to paddle.

Kayaking Canoeing Wildlife Scenic

Year-Round Beauty

Every Season Has Its Gift

There is no off-season at Blue Bear Cottage. Each season writes its own chapter.

🌿 Spring

  • 🌸 Wildflower trails emerging across the meadow
  • 🐦 Peak bird watching — warblers return in force
  • 💧 Waterfalls at their fullest from snowmelt
  • 🥾 Mud season hikes through softening trails
  • 🎣 Trout season opens — fish are hungry

☀️ Summer

  • 🫐 Wild blueberry picking across the open meadow
  • 🏊 Swimming at Great Sacandaga Lake, 2 miles away
  • 🌟 Adirondack stargazing at its peak clarity
  • 🥾 Long-day summit hikes with full trail access
  • 🎠 Saratoga Racing Season (July–September)

🍂 Fall

  • 🍁 Full Adirondack foliage from mid-September
  • 📸 Astrophotography with crisp, clear skies
  • 🦌 Wildlife active — deer, bear, hawk migrations
  • 🔥 Best fire tower views of the season
  • 🍎 Apple picking and local harvest in nearby towns

❄️ Winter

  • 🛷 Sledding and snow tubing on natural slopes
  • ⛷️ West Mountain Skiing, ~30 min away
  • ❄️ Snowshoeing through silent, snow-draped forest
  • 🌌 Winter Milky Way — dark skies at their sharpest
  • 🔥 Cozy cottage evenings by the fire
