A Real Off-Grid Escape

No Electricity. No Signal. Just the Wild.

Hiker Delight Homestead Cabin is a rustic loft cabin sitting on 80 acres of private mountain forest in the southern Adirondacks — two miles from Great Sacandaga Lake and a world away from everything else.

This is a true off-grid experience. The cabin has a wood cook stove for heat and cooking, a private composting toilet, a fire pit, and direct trail access from the front step. What it doesn't have: electricity, running water, Wi-Fi, or cell signal. Come knowing that — and you'll leave loving it.

Built and hosted by Jaquan, a Superhost and Black land owner who's spent years welcoming guests to this same 80-acre property. The cabin is designed for hikers, writers, stargazers, and anyone who's done a night without electricity before — and liked it.

Black-Owned True Off-Grid 80 Private Acres Year-Round Superhost

⚠️ Please read before booking: This is a camping experience in a cabin. Bring your own drinking water, headlamps, and food. Cell signal drops off on the property — download maps and let someone know where you're headed before you head up.
